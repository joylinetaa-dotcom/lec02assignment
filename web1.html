Term 13 — Web Evolution

Web 1.0

The first generation of the World Wide Web, characterised by static, read-only web pages with limited user interaction.

📷 Add your image: images/web1.jpg

DEFINITION

Web 1.0 refers to the first stage of the World Wide Web's evolution, covering roughly 1991 to 2004. It was a read-only web where content was created by webmasters and consumed passively by users, with no ability to interact, comment, or contribute content.

How It Works

Web 1.0 sites were simple HTML pages served from web servers. Pages were hand-coded and linked with hyperlinks. Users could read content and follow links but could not post comments, create profiles, or contribute any content of their own.

Early websites functioned essentially as digital brochures or information boards. There was no social media, no user accounts on most sites, and very little interactivity. Content rarely changed and was controlled entirely by the site owner or webmaster.

Era

Approximately 1991 to 2004 — the early years of the World Wide Web.

Nature

Static and read-only — users could only browse and read content.

Content Creator

Only webmasters and site owners could create and publish content.

User Interaction

Minimal — users could only browse pages and click hyperlinks.
