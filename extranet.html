Term 09 — Extended Networks

Extranet

A controlled private network that allows limited, authenticated access to an organisation's intranet resources by approved external parties.

📷 Add your image: images/extranet.jpg

DEFINITION

An extranet is an extension of an organisation's intranet that grants controlled access to specific external users such as business partners, suppliers, clients, or contractors. It uses Internet technologies but adds authentication layers to restrict access to approved parties only.

How It Works

Extranets extend the intranet beyond the organisation's boundaries. Authorised external users connect via a VPN or a dedicated secure web portal. The extranet firewall verifies their credentials and grants access only to the specific resources they are permitted to see.

Common uses include supplier order portals, client project dashboards, partner collaboration platforms, and healthcare systems where multiple organisations need to share patient data securely.

Who Can Access

Approved external parties such as suppliers, partners, and clients.

vs Intranet

An extranet extends intranet access beyond the organisation to trusted outsiders.

Security

Protected by VPN tunnels, SSL encryption, and user authentication.

Common Uses

Supplier portals, partner collaboration, client dashboards, and B2B platforms.
